Why I do this work

Technology earns its seat when it changes the number on the board’s page

I have spent my career in the operating core of organizations where the platform is the business. The hardest part is never the software. It is getting a room of capable executives to commit to one decision, then staying accountable until the result shows up. I take on the hard, multi-vendor, high-stakes programs others stall on, bring AI in where it removes real cost and risk, and stay close through go-live and benefits realization.

I am at my best when the stakes are high, the room is divided, and the outcome has to be real.

How I would operate

A 30-60-90 day plan

First 30 days

Listen and map

  • Meet the executive team, learn the goals and the friction firsthand
  • Read the existing stack, contracts, and roadmap before changing anything
  • Baseline the numbers: cost, risk, delivery health, where time leaks out
Days 31-60

Align and stabilize

  • Stand up light-but-real governance and a prioritized, funded roadmap
  • Fix the loudest operational and security gaps that erode trust
  • Pick one or two AI or automation wins with measurable payback
Days 61-90

Deliver and prove

  • Ship the first wins and report them in the board’s language
  • Set the multi-year platform and AI direction the whole table will defend
  • Establish the operating rhythm that keeps IT accountable to outcomes
